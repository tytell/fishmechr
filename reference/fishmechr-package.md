# fishmechr: Computes Kinematic Parameters for Swimming

Processes tracked points on a fish's body and uses them to estimate
standard kinematic parameters such as tail beat amplitude and frequency,
body wavelength and wavespeed. As part of this, it also estimates the
location of the center of mass and the principal swimming axis. The
techniques are described in detail in the main vignette and are
published in the book chapter Hawkins, O.H., Di Santo, V., Tytell,
Eric.D., 2025. "Biomechanics and energetics of swimming", in: Higham,
T.E., Lauder, G.V. (Eds.), Integrative Fish Biomechanics, Fish
Physiology. Academic Press.
[doi:10.1016/bs.fp.2025.06.003](https://doi.org/10.1016/bs.fp.2025.06.003)
.
